I didn't play LoL but I did try it out. It felt awkward to me. In DotA, just because your opponent is more skilled doesn't mean they'll beat you. Decision making, teamwork, tactics and strategy rule in DotA. A person who can hit every skill shot, maximize dps with picture perfect orb-walking, get every last hit and deny will still lose to a person bottle crowing and using aggression coupled with superior regen to nullify the skill advantage. Tactical decision making and having a long-term strategy(whether mid game timing, late game or early racks) nets you the win. Whereas LoL felt more actioney. It felt more like an action game to me where skill trumps anything.

LoL has far more strategies than you'd think. M5 has abused a number of them, where they took a champion's weakness and rolled it in that player's face every time. It does feel actiony because unlike in dota, Riot doesn't want infinite regen for heroes(exception always being irelia and warwick). This is good as they have the recall button to shorten travels.

Also, in LoL, if you see an opportunity you take it. In DotA, you might still be reluctant cause in DotA a champ has X ways of messing you up for good(not to mention the globals strat).

I've seen a lot of cool and unique strategies in LoL, and in DotA, just that in LoL you really have to know what to do in a split second while in DotA you may still have a bit more time for things to go off(i'd mainly combare teamfight lengths, as i recall in DotA, it is actually possible to mess an opponent up without going into a teamfight, while in LoL the leading team will mostly force them).
