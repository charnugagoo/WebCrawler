Dota Hero Analysis: Rooftrellen the Gentle Green Giant

Posted on Wed Jul 11, 2012 10:10 pm

Welcome to my number 76 hero analysis post which are located exclusively on Dotastrategy.com in the Heroes Forum. Today’s hero comes via special request of me, Ghoster88. Please focus on the hero’s abilities in your experience. Competitive vs Pub doesn’t matter, it’s just about the hero. All of the Analysis can be found in the Data Base which is located in The Library, or click the link located here. Hero Database


Rooftrellen the Treant Protector


Let’s start with his role and his stats. Rooftrellen is a support ganker. His aoe ultimate lends his skills to an initiator as well.

Stat Analysis

Strength=25+2.8 His starting STR is great and the growth is very good. Sadly, he used to have the highest growth in the game at 3.4, but thanks to Icefrog he no longer does.
Agility=15+2 The starting AGI is pretty poor but the growth is pretty solid. He only has 1 starting armor though.
Intelligence=17+1.8 Bad starting INT and a mediocre growth do little to ease his mana burdens.

Skill Analysis (Note: Abilities are discussed about their maximum level, unless stated otherwise.)

Nature’s Guise

The good- Rooftrellen shrouds himself in the deep leaves of the forest. Nature’s Guise will turn the target invisible for a whopping 60 seconds, provided they stay within a generous 375 aoe of a tree. The cd on this move is very short at 4 sec and the mana cost is only 60. This can be cast on any ally so Roof can cloak his entire team fairly easily and fairly quickly. Roof can also cast spells under Nature’s Guise without breaking the invisibility. Thematically this move is awesome. The idea of vines and branches covering up your friends to hide them from danger is awesome. As a final bonus, an ally blessed with the Guise will receive a 10% ms increase.

The bad- As stated earlier an ally will need to remain within distance of a tree to stay invisible. Despite all the trees on the map, it’s surprising how often this can be an issue. Another big issue is that there is a 2 sec fade time on the cloak, so you’ll be in danger while fading. Also, the cast range is only 300 so Roof has to be pretty close to a target to cloak them.

Leech Seed

The good- Roof throws a Pokeball at his enemies. Bulbasaur pops out and tags one foe with his patented Leech Seed. The enemy will be slowed by 24% for the next 3 sec. During those 3 sec, they will take 300 damage, divided into 4 pulses of 75 damage a piece. The interesting part is that just like the Pokemon move, those pulses will also heal Roof and any allied heroes or creeps by the same amount. The heal aoe is 500. It’s a good ganking tool, and it’s a great way to get health back while assaulting your foes. The cd is pretty reasonable at 12 sec too.

The bad- I don’t know what it is about slows, but this one often seems negligible. It’s definitely there, but it never seems to slow them as much as the number indicates it would. The mana cost is a brutal 140, even at level 1 which is retarded. Also, the heal/damage is over time, not instant. The cast range is 350 so again, Roof will have to be fairly close to tag someone with this.

Living Armor

The good- It’s a passive meaning it’s a relief for your mana pool. Huzzah. Roof will give any allied heroes in a 900 aoe 4 armor as well as 4 HP regen per sec. This also works on towers which is pretty handy. As a very nice bonus, the aura will be global for your allies during the daytime.

The bad- No flaws on this move.

Overgrowth

The good- Rooftrellen summons the Earth to do his bidding. Vines will sprout from the ground and ensare all enemies around Roof. The aoe is 625 around Roof. Enemies will be ensared for 3 sec. They won’t be able to move, attack, or cast the blink spell while held. They will also take 100 damage per sec resulting in 300 total. The mana cost is fairly cheap for a move like this at 200. The cd is also 95 sec, which again, is pretty good for an aoe ultimate hold like this. In a very strange twist, the vines will also go through Magic Immunity and hold the target.

The bad- In the second part of that strange twist, enemies who become Magic Immune after being ensnared will be free. So if you get caught, and hit MI, you’ll be free, but if you use MI, then get caught, you are stuck. It’s nice that Roof has a way to get around MI, but this seems needlessly inconsistent. Either it should totally goes through MI, or it doesn’t. Also(thanks to some recent nerfs) the DPS is pretty bad, especially the earlier levels( a pathetic 150 damage at level 1). 300 total damage for a level 3 ult like this is just sad. The worst part of Overgrowth is that it does not stop your opponents from casting spells. Casters can fully wreak havoc on you. It’s obnoxious. Overgrowth is also absurdly easy to counter. Any illusion creator or any fool with that dumb item Manta Style can simply pop out of the branches.

Overview- By the time I post this, Rooftrellen will have been altered in some way. Since I’ve been playing the game, he’s gone through about 4 or 5 reworks. He used to put wisps in trees to spy, then he created trees to spy, then he made owls to spy, now he doesn’t like to spy, cause it hurts his eye, cause he’s a gentle guy, so he saps health now. So forgive me if this analysis is obsolete once he is changed for the umpteenth time. Although regarding his most recent change, Rooftrellen is the most overpowered thing since sliced bread. Did you know that? I sure as hell didn’t. This hero has received nerf, after nerf, after nerf. I didn’t think he was all that powerful to begin with but apparently, he pissed off IceFrog. Rooftrellen use to be a lot of fun. He used to be a crazy tank because of how hard it was to get rid of him. He had the highest STR growth in the game, he had crazy armor properties, he hit fairly darn hard because of all that Strength. Now he is a sad pathetic shell of his former self. He almost has no identity because he’s been altered and changed so much at this point. I don’t know what his pub/comp popularity is but I can tell you for me personally I hate playing him now. I use to love him, particularly with the old Eyes in the Forest set up. Even his Sentinel set up was neat. And honestly I liked the simplicity of the Leech Seed set up as well but it instantly stopped being fun when he got nerfed into the ground. Personally, I can’t think of a single time when you should pick Roof over another hero of his type. His ult is far too easy to counter and since it no longer lasts 5 sec, it pales compared to Ravage or Black Hole. It’s so obnoxious to trap a team in Overgrowth, only to have 2 heroes pop BKB’s, two more cast stuns, and the last one hits their manta and look at that! Everyone is free. Now, Roof is definitely not useless. Leech Seed is a good ganking/healing tool. Nature’s Guise can be obnoxious to your enemies. Living Armor can be a great early game assist to your team(though it was horribly overrated, thus getting it nerfed). And Overgrowth can be ok, depending on the team you are fighting. Though he’s a shell of his former tanking self, Roof can still soak up a bit of damage. He can have some mana issues, but for the most part, his needs can be met fairly easily. But I really miss when this guy was awesome. Right now, he just makes me sad.

Response
Feel free to share your thoughts on Roof, line-ups you find him to be very strong in, and why you agree or disagree with my thoughts. And of course, the big obvious question, What is your favorite version of Rooftrellen? Personally I liked the old Eyes in the Forest version. The one where you put the wisps on the trees. Planting the trees themselves was equally nice. If you would like to see a certain hero looked at, let me know. Thanks for reading!

Verdict-

Need= Roof’s newest version was debuted in 6.72. We are currently on 6.74. This is every single nerf he received in that short time span.

-Armor reduced by 1
-Nature’s Guise cooldown increased from 3 seconds to 10/8/6/4 sec
-Leech Seed cooldown rescaled from 12 to 18/16/14/12 sec
-Living Armor now regenerates buildings only in a 900 aoe.
-Base armor reduced by 1, AGAIN!
-Added 2 second fade time to Nature’s Guise
-Overgrowth AOE reduced from 725 to 625
-Living Armor regeneration reduced from 2/3/4/5 to 1/2/3/4
-Strength from 27+3.4 to 25+2.8
-Overgrowth cooldown increased from 100/95/90 to 115/105/95
-Overgrowth damage per second decreased from 70/100/130 to 50/75/100
-Leech Seed manacost increased from 110/120/130/140 to 140.

Personally, I want every single one of those reverted. The only one that even made the slightest sense, was taking away the Global Tower Regen.

Grade= B-
